# California Car Insurance Guide 2024

California drivers pay an average of **$1,842 per year** for full coverage car insurance, but rates vary dramatically by city and driver profile.

## California Minimum Coverage Requirements
- **Bodily Injury Liability**: $15,000 per person / $30,000 per accident
- **Property Damage Liability**: $5,000 per accident  
- **Uninsured Motorist**: Required (matching your liability limits)

## Average Annual Premiums by City
1. **Los Angeles**: $2,100
2. **San Francisco**: $1,950
3. **San Diego**: $1,750
4. **Sacramento**: $1,650
5. **Fresno**: $1,600

## How to Save on California Car Insurance

### 1. California-Specific Discounts
- **Good Driver Discount**: Save 20% if no accidents or tickets in 3+ years
- **Low-Mileage Discount**: Up to 30% if you drive less than 7,500 miles/year
- **Prop 103 Savings**: Compare rates every 6 months (rates regulated)

### 2. Bundle for Extra Savings
- Bundle auto + home insurance: Save 15-25%
- Bundle auto + renters insurance: Save 10-15%
- California-specific: Bundle with earthquake insurance

### 3. Usage-Based Insurance Programs
- **Progressive Snapshot**: Save up to 30% for safe driving
- **Allstate Drivewise**: Average savings of 14%
- **State Farm Drive Safe & Save**: Up to 50% for top drivers

## Top Insurance Companies in California

2. **State Farm**: Best local agent network, average: $1,600/year  
3. **Progressive**: Best for high-risk drivers, average: $1,700/year

## California-Specific Programs
- **California Low-Cost Auto Insurance**: For qualified low-income drivers
- **Assigned Risk Plan**: For drivers who can't get coverage elsewhere
- **CA Good Driver Discount**: Automatic 20% discount for clean records
